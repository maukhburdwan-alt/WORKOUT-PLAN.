Muscle Gain — Workout & Diet

Important: This is general guidance for healthy adults. If you have medical conditions, injuries, or are older, consult a healthcare professional before starting.

Principles for Muscle Gain

  • Progressive overload: gradually increase weight, reps or sets.
  • Train compound movements (squats, deadlifts, presses, rows).
  • Recovery: sleep 7–9 hours and allow muscles 48–72 hours to recover between heavy sessions.
  • Nutrition: eat a small calorie surplus and prioritise protein.

Simple 3-Day Workout Split (Good for beginners → intermediate)

Do this plan 3 non-consecutive days per week (e.g., Mon / Wed / Fri). Each exercise: 3 sets of 6–12 reps (use weight that makes last reps challenging).

Warm-up (5–10 minutes)

  • Light cardio (walking / cycling) 3–5 min
  • Dynamic mobility: leg swings, arm circles, bodyweight squats — 5 min

Day 1 — Push (Chest / Shoulders / Triceps)

  • Barbell or dumbbell bench press — 3 sets x 6–10 reps
  • Overhead press (dumbbell or barbell) — 3 x 6–10
  • Incline dumbbell press or push-ups — 3 x 8–12
  • Triceps dips or overhead triceps extension — 3 x 8–12
  • Optional: 10 minutes light cardio after workout

Day 2 — Pull (Back / Biceps)

  • Deadlift or Romanian deadlift — 3 x 5–8 (if trained; otherwise substitute with hip hinge and good mornings)
  • Barbell or dumbbell row — 3 x 6–10
  • Lat pulldown or assisted pull-up — 3 x 6–10
  • Biceps curls (bar or dumbbell) — 3 x 8–12

Day 3 — Legs & Core

  • Squats (barbell or goblet) — 3 x 6–10
  • Lunges or split squats — 3 x 8–12 each leg
  • Leg curl or glute bridge — 3 x 8–12
  • Calf raises — 3 x 12–15
  • Plank — 3 x 30–60 seconds

Progression & Rest

  • Increase weight when you can do the top of rep range for all sets.
  • Rest 60–120 seconds between heavy sets; 30–60s for accessory work.
  • Optional active recovery day: walking, stretching, yoga.

Basic Diet Guidelines for Muscle Gain

  • Calories: Eat a small surplus (about +250–500 kcal/day) above maintenance.
  • Protein: Aim for ~1.6–2.2 g per kg body weight per day (e.g., 70 kg → 112–154 g protein).
  • Carbohydrates: Fuel workouts — moderate to high (3–6 g/kg depending on activity).
  • Fats: 20–30% of total calories, include healthy fats (olive oil, nuts, seeds, fish).
  • Timing: Eat protein every 3–4 hours; include a protein-rich meal or shake within 1–2 hours after training.
  • Hydration: Drink water throughout the day; more around workouts.

Sample Day (Approximate)

  • Breakfast: Oats with milk, banana, and 2 scoops of Greek yogurt or whey protein.
  • Snack: Handful of nuts + fruit.
  • Lunch: Grilled chicken / tofu, brown rice or chapati, large salad or steamed veg.
  • Pre-workout snack (30–60 min): Toast with peanut butter or banana.
  • Post-workout: Protein shake or chicken + rice.
  • Dinner: Fish or paneer, sweet potato or whole grain, vegetables.
  • Before bed (optional): Cottage cheese / greek yogurt (slow-digesting protein).

Supplements (Optional)

  • Creatine monohydrate (~3–5 g/day) — well-studied for strength (consult doctor if kidney issues).
  • Protein powder — convenient to reach protein goals.
  • Multivitamin / fish oil if diet lacks variety.

Safety & Practical Tips

  • Focus on form before adding weight — poor technique causes injury.
  • If new to lifting, consider a trainer for 2–4 sessions to learn movements.
  • Track workouts (weight, sets, reps) to ensure progressive overload.
  • If you have joint problems or are older, choose lower-impact variations and reduce load; consult a professional.

Good luck — stay consistent, patient, and track progress. Small steady gains add up.
