Cardio Patients — Exercise & Diet Plan

Important: This plan is for general heart-healthy guidance. Always follow your doctor’s recommendations and avoid over-exertion.

Precautions for Cardio Patients

  • Warm up at least 5–10 minutes before exercise.
  • Avoid heavy lifting or high-intensity workouts unless approved by a doctor.
  • Stop immediately if you feel chest pain, dizziness, breathlessness, or extreme fatigue.
  • Keep water nearby and stay hydrated.
  • Do not exercise in extreme heat or cold.

Recommended Exercises

Focus on **low-intensity and moderate-intensity** activities that support heart health.

Daily Activity Plan (Simple & Safe)

  • Walking: 20–30 minutes at a comfortable pace
  • Light Cycling: 10–20 minutes (indoor preferred)
  • Stretching & Mobility: 10 minutes
  • Breathing Exercises: 5–10 minutes

Weekly Exercise Schedule

  • Monday: Brisk Walk + Light Stretching
  • Tuesday: Yoga (light) + Breathing Exercises
  • Wednesday: Walk + Light Resistance Band Work
  • Thursday: Flexibility & Mobility Training
  • Friday: Walk or Cycling
  • Saturday: Deep Breathing + Gentle Yoga
  • Sunday: Rest or slow walking

Exercises to Avoid

  • Heavy weight lifting
  • High-intensity interval training (HIIT)
  • Fast running or sprinting
  • Skipping rope
  • Sudden, jerky movements

Diet Plan for Cardio Patients

  • Eat low-salt, low-fat, heart-safe foods.
  • Include whole grains, fruits, and leafy vegetables.
  • Prefer lean proteins: fish, egg whites, tofu, dal.
  • Avoid deep-fried foods, red meat, butter, and excess oil.
  • Limit sugar and refined carbs.
  • Drink adequate water daily.

Sample Heart-Healthy Day

  • Breakfast: Oats + fruit + 1 egg white
  • Lunch: Brown rice/roti + dal + steamed vegetables
  • Snack: Fruit or nuts (unsalted)
  • Dinner: Soup + vegetables + lean protein
  • Before bed: Warm water or herbal tea

Important Safety Tips

  • Check your heart rate—exercise should feel “comfortable”.
  • Don’t hold your breath during any exercise.
  • Take small breaks if you feel tired.
  • Always carry prescribed medicines (if any).

Stay active safely — your heart will thank you!
