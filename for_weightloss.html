Weight Loss — Workout & Diet

Note: General guidance for healthy adults. If you have medical conditions (heart, diabetes, joint issues, etc.) consult a healthcare professional before starting.

Principles for Weight Loss

  • Create a moderate calorie deficit (typically 300–500 kcal/day) — not extreme.
  • Combine regular exercise (cardio + strength) with protein-rich, fibre-rich diet.
  • Consistency, sleep (7–9 hrs) and hydration are key.
  • Prefer whole foods and avoid sugary drinks & deep-fried snacks.

Weekly Workout Overview

Aim for 4–6 days/week depending on fitness level. Mix cardio, strength, and mobility.

Sample 5-Day Plan (repeat weekly)

  • Day 1 — Full body strength (45 min)
    • Warm-up: 5–8 min brisk walk or light cycling
    • Squats (bodyweight or goblet) — 3 sets x 10–15
    • Push-ups (inclined if needed) — 3 x 8–12
    • Bent-over rows (dumbbell or band) — 3 x 8–12
    • Glute bridges — 3 x 12–15
    • Plank — 3 x 20–45 sec
  • Day 2 — Cardio & Core (30–45 min)
    • Interval walk/jog or cycling: 5 min easy, then 20–25 min intervals (1–2 min faster + 1–2 min easy)
    • Core circuit: Bicycle crunches, leg raises, side plank — 2 rounds
  • Day 3 — Lower body strength + low cardio (40 min)
    • Lunges — 3 x 10 each leg
    • Step-ups — 3 x 10 each leg
    • Hamstring curl (stability ball or lying) or single-leg deadlift — 3 x 10–12
    • 10–15 min steady walk after strength
  • Day 4 — Active recovery / Mobility (30 min)
    • Gentle yoga, stretching and balance work
    • Light 20–30 min walk
  • Day 5 — Full body circuit (30–40 min)
    • Perform circuit 3 times with 60–90 sec rest between rounds:
    • 10 squats, 10 push-ups, 15 kettlebell/dumbbell swings (or hip hinge), 15 bent-over rows, 30 sec mountain climbers
  • Optional Day 6 — Longer steady cardio (45–60 min)
    • Brisk walk, cycling, swimming or hiking at moderate pace
  • Day 7 — Rest
    • Full rest or very light activity (short walk, stretching)

Progression & Tips

  • Increase intensity slowly: longer intervals, more reps, or slightly heavier weights.
  • Focus on form — quality > quantity.
  • Track workouts and food for at least a few weeks to understand progress.

Diet Guidelines for Weight Loss

  • Eat a moderate calorie deficit (approx. 300–500 kcal less than maintenance).
  • Protein target: ~1.2–2.0 g/kg body weight per day to preserve muscle.
  • Fill plate with vegetables, lean protein, whole grains or legumes, and healthy fats.
  • Limit added sugars, refined carbs, and fried foods. Prefer whole fruits over juices.
  • Eat regular meals and healthy snacks to avoid extreme hunger and overeating.
  • Hydrate: 6–10 glasses of water daily (more if active).

Sample Day (Weight Loss)

  • Breakfast: Oats with skim/plant milk, a handful of berries, and a spoon of peanut/almond butter.
  • Mid-morning snack: Greek yogurt (or plain yogurt) or a small handful of nuts.
  • Lunch: Grilled chicken/tofu, large salad with olive oil & lemon, 1 small whole grain (brown rice / chapati).
  • Afternoon snack: Fruit + a boiled egg or roasted chana.
  • Dinner: Vegetable soup or stir-fry with lean protein and lots of veggies; keep carbs smaller at dinner.
  • Optional (if hungry): Cottage cheese or a small protein snack before bed.

Smart Habits

  • Prefer home-cooked meals so you control ingredients and portions.
  • Use smaller plates to help portion control.
  • Plan meals and prepare snacks to avoid impulse eating.
  • Limit alcohol — it adds empty calories and reduces willpower.

Safety & Special Notes

  • Start slow if new to exercise; build up duration and intensity over weeks.
  • If you have joint pain, choose low-impact cardio (cycling, swimming, elliptical).
  • Stop and seek help if you experience chest pain, fainting, severe dizziness or shortness of breath.
  • Consult a dietitian for personalised calorie targets or if you have medical conditions.

Consistency, patience and small sustainable changes win. Good luck — stay active and enjoy the process!
